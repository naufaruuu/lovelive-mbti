background:
Kosuzu applied to the School Idol Club because she admired Sayaka Murano in particular. She lives in the city of Tsuruga, and is the youngest in a family of ten (though they're not all siblings, her grandparents and cousins live with them as well). She described herself as being "useless" at home, saying that she tried to help out with the fishing back in elementary school but got hurt pretty badly in the process. Ever since then, her family has treated her like a doll: someone fragile that they need to protect.

She has grown up believing that she has never done a single amazing thing in her life, working hard but never achieving any results. This drive is likely brought on her poor self-image, as well as her determination to do her best as a school idol.

personality:
Kosuzu first appears as a frazzled, panicked girl who believes she has never achieved anything. She has an extremely low self-esteem, putting herself down and stating things like, "It'd be better if I wasn't around". She constantly worries if she's bothering others, calling herself a shameless good for nothing that "has only ever been a burden to others". It is for this reason that she makes up seemingly pointless goals, such as crossing the lake, in order to feel like she's accomplished something. But despite the many goals she makes for herself, she doesn't believe that any will come true, stating that "there isn't the tiniest fraction of a chance that everything will go perfectly".

But as she gets to know Tsuzuri and Sayaka, she is instilled with a new drive: to finally become something as a member of DOLLCHESTRA. Sayaka, recognizing her will power as being similar to her own, respects her relentless motivation and hard work, likening her to a hard-working, bright yellow color.

To pump herself up, she shouts "Chesuto!!", a phrase which she got from her grandfather. She tends to freeze up and speak a mile a minute when nervous, but speaks very matter-of-factly and to the point. Kosuzu is eager to challenge new things, despite expecting to fail, and isn't afraid to keep trying no matter how often she has to.

Kosuzu's biggest weakness is giving up before she's even started something, and is obvlious to the fact she doesn't get the difference between courage and recklessness. However, her biggest strength is that since she spends so much time watching and being envious of others, she's good at identifying people's strengths. She's also good at complimenting people and making them feel more confident about themselves.

(anibase.net)

Kosuzu speaks clearly and loudly, but often sounds as if she is trying very hard not to lose her nerve.

Even the way she ends livestreams has a lot of force.

Because her voice is so naturally loud, viewers repeatedly requested that she appear in an ASMR stream.

When that finally happened in June of the 105th class year with Ceras Yanagida Lilienfeld, the contrast was hilarious: Ceras whispered softly into one ear, while Kosuzu was simply being her normal loud self in the other.

Her everyday speech is youthful and slightly androgynous, yet she also likes using grand and old-fashioned expressions, especially toward seniors.

She may call herself useless or say she is no good at all, but she says these things with such brightness that it creates a unique mix of negativity and optimism.

Ginko describes her as someone with an unguarded, friendly warmth.

Kosuzu often teams up with Hime to tease Ginko, only to get scolded in return.

She tends to agree with almost any suggestion from her seniors.

She also blurts out absurd comments with cheerful confidence, though she will step in and play the straight man when Sayaka gets truly out of hand.

She calls classmates and younger students by their given names with affectionate familiarity.

She calls seniors by surname plus "senior," refers to the 102nd class club members as "great seniors," and even calls Sachi from the 101st class a "super senior."

She likes making little catchphrases out of her family name, such as "the rock-solid Kachimachi."

Her future dream is, quite literally, to become bigger, ideally reaching two or even four meters tall.

She struggles academically.

Her goal for her first-term final exams was simply to avoid failing grades, largely because she is sincere but disorganized and often falls behind on assignments.

She can also be surprisingly mischievous.

She has joked about doing homework for others, answering roll call in someone else's place, forging outing forms, and sneaking late-night food in the kitchen.

As the child of a fishing family, she is excellent at identifying fish.


At Omicho Market, she successfully named every fish species she was shown.

She is comfortable with fishing and seeing fish prepared for cooking.

Back home, she spent summers catching crayfish.

She loves fish dishes in general and also loves ramune candy.

Ironically, she is not good at cooking.

Like many people from Fukui, she adores dinosaurs.

Her bag, clothes, and personal items often feature dinosaur motifs, and the emoji most commonly used to represent her is a dinosaur.

Her hobby of collecting stationery appears in livestreams where she excitedly introduces pens to others such as Megumi Fujishima, Ginko Momose, and Hime Anyoji.

She is also a talented artist and can draw cute icon-like portraits of the members.

Kosuzu is more open to scary stories than one might expect.

During a ghost-story challenge stream, she even delivered a surprisingly atmospheric performance and ended by staring into empty space.

Strengths and Weaknesses
Her biggest hidden strength is noticing the strengths of other people.

This was highlighted in the 104th class fifth activity record, where it became clear that her habit of envying others had sharpened her ability to recognize what makes them shine.

She can praise others naturally and effectively.

That skill even helps motivate strong personalities like Kaho Hinoshita and Megumi Fujishima.

When talking about the people she admires, especially Sayaka and Kaho, she can become an incredibly fast-talking fan.

By her second year, she had finally started acknowledging that finding other people's strengths really is her special skill.

Her weaknesses are also very Kosuzu-like.

She often fails to notice other people's flaws, and she is so unafraid of failure that she sometimes takes reckless risks.

Kosuzu's major personal project is the Kachimachi Challenge, a long-running goal to attempt many different challenges during her three years of high school.

She started it because she wanted something she could truly be proud of.

The emotional root of this project lies in her family life.

She felt frustrated by being treated like a child for years, though she later reflected that this overprotection came from a childhood incident in which she nearly suffered a serious injury while trying to help with fishing work on her own.

Before entering Hasu no Sora, her mother told her to stay safely at home.

Kosuzu instead rebelled, took action almost on impulse, and applied to the school because she wanted to stop being a person who had never achieved anything.

People around her also influenced this mindset.

In particular, her old friend Yukika and the cousin she calls "older sister to the side" inspired her through the ways they chose to live.

Her challenges range from major goals like dancing well or placing in competitions to tiny ones like cooking, speed-eating, catching cherry blossom petals, or even finding a better way to go down a slide.


She often fails, but she keeps trying, sometimes to the point of exhaustion or physical danger.

Fear of Parting and Fear of Failure

By the eighth activity record of the 104th class year, Tsuzuri has just finished career experience activities and begun seeing a possible future path.

That makes the reality of graduation hit Kosuzu hard.

A casual question about whether Tsuzuri would take home a peking duck item during the clubroom cleanup triggers Kosuzu's panic over the coming separation.

She runs out of the room, overwhelmed by the thought of losing the place and people who made her into a school idol.

Kosuzu is the first to find Tsuzuri afterward.

She tries a new challenge: making Tsuzuri smile.

It works for a moment, but Tsuzuri admits that she can smile only because Kosuzu is there.

Kosuzu, unable to bear the idea of Tsuzuri crying, screams into empty space as if trying to stop time itself.

Sayaka and Tsuzuri later clash emotionally over the future.

Kosuzu even steps in to stop them, though the exchange eventually helps Tsuzuri realize that the future still holds possibilities.

Kosuzu tells her that even catching hold of someone who will stay beside you is itself a challenge.

She gives Tsuzuri the peking duck item and later joins a loud-voice contest where she shouts once more for time to stop, using the moment to process her sadness.

In the tenth activity record of the 104th class year, just before the Love Live finals, Kosuzu trains alone instead of pairing up with anyone.

She confesses to Kaho that she is terrified because this is a stage where she cannot afford to fail.

Kaho first sympathizes, then later reframes the issue for her.

If trying your best is fun, then any stage can become fun too.

Kosuzu takes that to heart.

She concludes that she has never once wanted to fail in any live, and that even if things do not go perfectly, she wants to give everything she can in that moment.

Kosuzu often incorporates two signature gestures into choreography.

One is the pose tied to her "Chestoo!" shout, and the other is a claw-like "roar" pose that matches her playful dinosaur image.

She works extremely hard and rarely complains during practice.

Her challenge mentality means she is willing to throw herself into repeated attempts until she gets results.

Her school bag badge collection is one of her clearest personal symbols.

She does not add a badge for every small challenge, only for major ones that give her a huge sense of accomplishment.

By October of the 104th class year, the number of badges on her bag had grown from two in May to seven.

After forming the Little Triangle, the trio also attached matching peace-sign keychains in different colors.

She often fails, but she keeps trying, sometimes to the point of exhaustion or physical danger.

Story Role
Joining DOLLCHESTRA

Kosuzu first appears in the first activity record of the 104th class year.

She arrives at Hasu no Sora as someone who has never done anything "amazing" and decides that she must accomplish at least one thing there.

Almost immediately, she attempts to cross the school's lake on a raft and fails.

Sayaka Murano and Tsuzuri Yugiri happen to be there and rescue her.

What changes everything is not just the rescue, but Sayaka's response.

For the first time, someone listens to Kosuzu's silly challenge stories without laughing at her.

Encouraged by Sayaka, Kosuzu tries again and eventually succeeds on her own.

This matters deeply to her because, until then, she had always given up quickly and moved on to something else.

On the way back to the dormitory, Tsuzuri tells her that Sayaka is a school idol.

Kosuzu then tracks down the School Idol Club by herself and declares that she wants to join.

When Sayaka gives her a homemade "Lake Crossing" badge, Kosuzu decides on a new dream.

Within three years, she wants to cover her school bag with badges earned through major personal achievements.

Tsuzuri had imagined DOLLCHESTRA's ideal new member as someone like "a bright yellow who would come of her own will."

Kosuzu fits that image perfectly and becomes the new member of DOLLCHESTRA.

Tsuruga Performance and Family Recognition

In the second activity record of the 104th class year, Kosuzu's family still responds to her school idol news with worry rather than praise.

They ask if she is forcing herself or causing trouble for others.

Then a school idol event is announced in her hometown of Tsuruga.

Kosuzu turns it into a challenge: she wants to show her family a version of herself they can be proud of.

She asks Sayaka to train her.

Sayaka hesitates at first because she has never properly coached someone before, but eventually agrees.

Kosuzu chooses Sayaka as her teacher for one simple reason.


She wants to become more like the senior she admires most.

At that point, Kosuzu lacks stamina, balance, precision, and the composure to look out at the audience during a performance.

After one week of Sayaka's coaching, she improves dramatically.

A live performance in Fukui seems to prove that the training worked.

Kosuzu completes her part smoothly and confidently.

But when Sayaka watches the recording later, she falls into despair.

She realizes Kosuzu has copied Sayaka's style too closely, and in doing so, Kosuzu's own feelings have disappeared from the performance.

This leads to a painful misunderstanding.

When Kosuzu tearfully asks if admiring Sayaka was itself a mistake, Sayaka is so shaken by her own past that she momentarily fails to deny it.

The turning point comes when Kosuzu stages a solo guerrilla performance at the venue the next day.

She trips, makes mistakes, and gives a messy performance, but this time her feelings come through clearly.

That imperfect stage shines in Sayaka's eyes.

Sayaka realizes that admiration itself was never wrong and promises to remain someone Kosuzu can continue looking up to.

At the Tsuruga event, DOLLCHESTRA performs a new song whose lyrics Sayaka wrote especially for Kosuzu.

For the first time, her family tells her she was amazing, and the new badge she earns bears the words "great success."

Later, Kosuzu mentions that her family now watches recordings together.

She has even held family calls that included Sayaka and Tsuzuri.

The Film for Yukika

The fifth activity record of the 104th class year focuses on Kosuzu's childhood friend Yukika.

Yukika dreamed of becoming a film director and used to upload self-made movies online.

Kosuzu saw her as the opposite of herself, someone talented who could do anything.

Yet Yukika admired Kosuzu in return, praising her ability to keep going no matter how often she failed.

Then Yukika sends news that she is giving up her dream because of family circumstances and moving overseas.

She has even deleted all of her uploaded films.

Kosuzu cannot accept that ending.

To tell Yukika that challenging yourself is never wrong, she decides to make an independent film about "Kosuzu trying her best."

At first she misunderstands Sayaka's lesson about self-expression and thinks that relying on others is forbidden.


She attempts to do everything alone, fails repeatedly, and nearly keeps pushing on anyway until Sayaka and Tsuzuri persuade her to ask the whole club for help.

The eight members then come together for a training-camp-style production at Kozue Otomune's villa.

Kozue handles scheduling, Sayaka Murano and Megumi Fujishima do filming, Kaho Hinoshita and Hime Anyoji write the script, Rurino Osawa and Hime Anyoji edit, Ginko Momose handles costumes, Rurino finds locations, and Sayaka with Rurino takes care of cooking.

Kosuzu plays the protagonist "Kosuzu."

Ginko plays the heroine "Ginko," Tsuzuri plays the rival "Tsuzuri," and Sayaka plays Tsuzuri's senior "Sayaka."

The film keeps the performers' real names but gives them alternate personalities and speech styles.

In the movie, Kosuzu uses a tougher "I" and comes across as more boyish than usual.

Filming is rough, and many retakes are caused by Kosuzu's mistakes.

However, during an outdoor scene in the rain, her spontaneous improvisation and Ginko's response create a genuinely excellent shot.

A new crisis appears when Sayaka develops a fever that night.

After talking with Tsuzuri, Kosuzu comes up with a way to change the ending and continue filming.

The completed movie reaches Yukika.

In response, Yukika sends back a video and seems to promise that her own dream will continue after all.

This experience becomes the beginning of Kosuzu's determination to aim for victory in Love Live.

For her, filmmaking also becomes a way to preserve school idol memories in a concrete form.

The Little Triangle Trio

In the seventh activity record of the 104th class year, Kosuzu grows closer to her fellow first years Ginko Momose and Hime Anyoji.

The three are vital to the full-group song Link to the FUTURE, but they keep making mistakes in unit coordination and formation practice.

During training, they receive advice from transfer student Izumi Katsuragi.

She tells them to identify one another's strengths and weaknesses.

Kosuzu's strength in dance is the size and boldness of her performance.

It is the result of her effort not to look small even beside Tsuzuri and Sayaka.

The trio then helps run a sweet shop and joins the club stage at the school festival.

Both attempts end in major failure.

Kosuzu blames herself, but things become more complicated when Izumi tells her that Ginko and Hime are the only ones truly at fault.

Kosuzu becomes furious.

Later, it is revealed that Izumi had separately told Ginko that Kosuzu takes actions while unconsciously assuming failure is acceptable.

Her fearlessness can become recklessness, especially because she lacks enough experience with one-chance-only stages.


Ginko fears failure too much and overthinks how she appears to others.

Hime had already sensed both of their weaknesses, but past trauma kept her from speaking up.

Kosuzu then realizes that she should have been the first to admit what truly mattered to her.

For Love Live, failure is not something she can casually gamble with.

Once the three honestly acknowledge each other's flaws, their practice improves dramatically.

They begin calling themselves the Little Triangle of Hasu no Sora, hoping to one day build the kind of bond their seniors have.
