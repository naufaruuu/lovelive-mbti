(idol.st)

desc 1:
A first year at Hasunosora Girls’ High. She loves school idols, and is always thinking about them, even during class. She craves attention and is a bit clingy. Originally from Nagano, she was friends with Kaho Hinoshita from their time in the hospital together, and call each other “hospi-pals.” She enjoys playing the violin, but according to her, it’s nothing more than a hobby.

background:
Ceras is half-German. She dreamed of being an expert violinist, but because of her weak constitution she could barely attend lessons. She was frequently in and out of the hospital when she was younger, where she met and became good friends with Kaho Hinoshita. However, after Kaho's discharge, she became increasingly lonely without anyone to talk to. This is when the Mizukawa School Idol Club came to the hospital to perform. She was stubborn and standoffish at first, not being able to relate to their songs about achieving your dreams because she believed that even if she picked up the violin again, she would never catch up to her peers. But things changed for her when they invited her to be a school idol: a dream anyone can realize regardless of where they are in life.

She met Izumi on a rainy day outside of Izumi's school grounds, located in Tokyo. Ceras confided in her that her school was closing, and that she needed to win Love Live! to save it. She had been to almost 300 schools at that point, trying and failing to recruit people to join the school idol club. Izumi almost instantly agreed to help, offering to transfer schools just to win for her. In return for this, Ceras agreed to give her "everything".

She was unable to perform in Love Live! initially, but thanks to Kaho's petition, Ceras was allowed to perform alongside Izumi Katsuragi.

personality:
Ceras is a soft-spoken but determined girl who dreams of being a school idol. She is carefree and likes to joke around with her childhood friend, Kaho. The two are still very close, with Ceras calling her "Hana-chan" and Kaho calling her "Secchan". Despite Kaho being her rival in the competition, she remains friendly towards her, inviting her over to her school to tell her all about her goals. She's glad when Kaho succeeds but still wishes to come out on top in the competition, letting the two have a healthy rivalry. She is an extremely hard worker, going above and beyond to ensure that her dream comes true. Due to her time as an official club member, she manages others well and is very capable for her age.

Kaho says that she exaggerates a lot to try and get people's attention. She also has quite a teasing side, enjoying getting people like Ginko riled up.

(anibase.net)

Ceras often presents herself as refined and graceful, but she becomes much more clingy and casual with people she gets close to.

She likes exaggerated jokes, absurd lies, theatrical voices, and playful overstatements.

She will casually turn "two years" into "two hundred years" or "less than thirty minutes" into "three days."

She also invents ridiculous stories, such as schools being attacked by wyverns or herself and Kaho being scouted by major hospitals because they were too cute.

Because she was praised as doll-like from a young age, she has very high confidence in her looks.

She frequently introduces herself with overblown phrases like "mysterious beauty" or "lovely beautiful girl" without embarrassment.

She sometimes compares herself to an angel, especially when contrasted with Izumi.

If she likes someone, she can become endlessly attached, and Izumi comments that the best way to handle her is to brush her off lightly.

Her bright and joking persona is partly deliberate.

She developed it during long hospital stays because she wanted people to pay attention to her.

In truth, she is lonely, clingy, and somewhat shy.

She can start conversations, but she has trouble deepening friendships, so stuffed animals once served as her companions.

Even with close friends, she needs time to lower her guard.

When she shouted loudly in January of her third middle school year, Kaho was surprised because she had apparently never heard Ceras raise her voice before.

During the Mizukawa period of the story, she spoke more calmly because she was under emotional strain.

By the Hasu no Sora period, her speech became looser, brighter, and more openly mischievous.

At heart, she is serious, considerate, and aware of social boundaries.

When she realizes someone is genuinely wounded, she avoids joking and becomes very attentive.

This appears especially in how she handled Izumi's emotional scars and fear during a horror-themed event.

When a horror film gathering was suggested, Ceras paid careful attention to Izumi's comfort rather than teasing her.

She is weak under television cameras due to a childhood trauma.

When surrounded by cameras, she becomes nervous and cannot fully show her abilities.

Streaming is easier for her.

In With x MEETS, she speaks much more naturally.

She was scouted by the drama club together with Izumi.

Despite being a complete amateur, she was cast as a rival magical girl in "Magical Girl Rhythmic Heart 3" because she matched the role so well.

She required many retakes, but she discovered that acting was fun.

She hopes to join future productions too.

Tastes and quirks
Ceras loves horror, especially panic horror.

Unlike Kaho, who is easily frightened, Ceras enjoys ghost stories, scary topics, zombie works, and even shark movies.

Official comic material shows that she loved scaring Kaho with horror stories since childhood.

She repeatedly watched the 104th term ghost-story stream and later helped create more spooky content in the 105th term.

She loves food and talks about it with great enthusiasm.

Corn is a particular favorite, and she can be oddly specific about the ideal way to chill and eat it.

She named one of Sayaka's vegetable-themed cushions "Cornfield."

She likes sea otters, dislikes cicadas intensely, and wishes for their extinction.

Her whiteboard drawings are memorable for their unusual style.

She gives people sharp chins and dramatic eyelashes, and when she draws Izumi, the chin becomes especially exaggerated.

A recurring With x MEETS habit of hers is beginning with a cheerful "Waa."

She also enjoys punchline openings, wordplay on her own very long name, and internet-style jokes in thumbnails and streams.

Her full name has fourteen syllabic beats in Japanese counting, which inspired a stream gimmick using it as the ending phrase of a tanka poem.

She inherited the club's end-of-stream imaginary wristwatch pose, but made it her own by doing a double-wristwatch version with both arms.

She likes games, though she mostly played alone before joining the club.

Like many of her seniors, she tends to neglect studying because she is too busy thinking about school idols.

Her test performance is therefore a recurring concern.

She also procrastinated heavily on summer homework and even found a crumpled blank sheet after the second term had already begun.

School idol ideals and artistic ability
Ceras's devotion to school idol activity is described as intense enough to burn with her life.

When she believes in a goal, she does everything she can.

At Mizukawa, she supported Izumi in every way available to a middle schooler.

She handled scouting, songwriting, massage, guidance on school idol basics, and practical setup for streaming and event appearances.

At Hasu no Sora, she initially prepared custom self-introductions for all three traditional units because she wanted to fit whichever one she joined.

All of them were a little off in their own way, though one fiery phrase became especially memorable.

She is the lyricist and composer of Edel Note.

Because of her violin background and familiarity with classical music, she can write across a wide range of styles.

She considered herself quite capable at composition from the beginning.

Her entrance to Hasu no Sora was earned through a special talent examination emphasizing lyrics, composition, and singing.

By the 105th term, Izumi had learned enough by working beside her that Ceras could entrust some lyrics to her.

This happened on songs such as "Sixteenth Night Selene" and "Fusion Crust."

Her old relationship with the violin was painful because it represented the dream illness took away from her.

School idols helped her transform that pain, and she can now play violin purely as a joyful hobby.

Before joining Hasu no Sora, she had already watched all available live performances and With x MEETS archives from the 103rd and 104th terms.

When a karaoke-themed voting event chose Cerise Bouquet as the unit style that suited her best, she named "Special Thanks," "Moonlit Jellyfish," and "Song of Splendor" as songs she wanted to sing.

For all her devotion to others, she often suppresses her own wishes.

Izumi even calls her twisted or contrary because she hides her own dream while dedicating herself to helping others.

She inherited the old Mizukawa school idol motto, "school idols who make everyone's dreams come true."

Around July of the 105th term, she defined the ideal school idol she wanted to become as "a school idol who fulfills someone else's dream."

Hasu no Sora, despite being in position to win by default, insisted that they wanted to earn victory properly and had no wish to leave regrets behind.

Kaho and the others therefore offered to help overturn the suspension order and make the playoff happen.

Kaho proposed staging a final cultural festival at Mizukawa.

By mobilizing local residents, graduates, and everyone connected to the school, they hoped to create support and attract attention to Mizukawa's situation.

Hasu no Sora made repeated trips to Nagano, and Ceras and Izumi worked at the center of the preparations.

A television crew came to cover the effort, which triggered Ceras's old trauma from a childhood violin interview where fear had made her play badly.

Encouraged by advice from Kosuzu and by the idea that failure can be used for growth, Ceras pushed herself forward.

Her casual talk may still have stumbled, but by the day of the event she successfully stood before many reporters.

During a live-stage interview at the festival itself, she delivered a dignified speech of thanks.

She also spontaneously coined the event's name, the Mizuren Festival.

The festival's success brought public sympathy, support, and donations.

It was not enough to save the school entirely, but it delayed the enforcement of the suspension order by one month and made the playoff possible.

Ceras thanked Kaho, saying that every club at Mizukawa would now be able to say goodbye properly.

During this process, she also deepened her bonds with the members of Hasu no Sora.

Ceras's hidden dream revealed
After submitting a report to the student council, Ceras checked supportive comments from people backing the effort to reverse the club suspension.

One comment from an older Mizukawa school idol alumna she called "big sister" referred to both Mizukawa's closure and "Ceras's dream" as sad losses.

Kaho noticed Ceras trying to brush this off.

Determined to understand, she and the others searched through old Mizukawa streams that had become more visible thanks to the festival's publicity.

They found a broadcast from two years earlier, shortly after Ceras entered Mizukawa middle school and was recruited by three older girls into the school idol club.

When asked about her future dream, Ceras had answered that she wanted to stand on the Love Live stage someday as a member of Mizukawa Girls'.

When Kaho confronted her, Ceras denied it and insisted that her dream was simply for Mizukawa to win through Izumi.

Izumi, however, told the truth: Ceras's dream had never changed, and she had always simply wanted to appear in Love Live herself as a girl who loved school idols.

Because Mizukawa had been closed before she could become a high school student there, that dream was now impossible under normal rules.

Even so, Ceras confessed that she at least wanted people to remember Mizukawa, wanted Izumi to fight without regret, and wanted Kaho to keep smiling.

The movement to place her on the Love Live stage
Moved by everything Ceras had done for her and by Ceras's own wish to "bloom," Kaho decided to fight for Ceras's dream in return.

She proposed using "the collective will of school idols across the nation" to break through a wall that should have been impossible to cross.

Kaho and the others contacted school idols around the country and appealed through streams for support and signatures.

Ceras was deeply grateful that Kaho would struggle for her until the very end.

Eventually the tournament organizers replied.

The playoff would proceed as planned, and if Hasu no Sora agreed, Ceras Yanagida Lilienfeld would be allowed to participate as a special exception.

This was unprecedented.

Rather than a special transfer case or someone retroactively treated as a high school student, an ordinary middle schooler was permitted to stand as a school idol in the Love Live tournament.

By refusing to give up and by being supported by friends and fans, Ceras finally reached her dream.

She would sing on the Love Live stage as a school idol of Mizukawa.

The playoff and joining Edel Note onstage
On the day of the playoff, Ceras became the second member of what had previously been Izumi's solo unit, Edel Note.

For the stage, Izumi wore a knight-like outfit while Ceras wore a white princess-style dress.

The story does not specify how much practice she managed in such a short time.

Even so, she stood confidently beside Izumi and performed "Edelied" with striking composure.

After Edel Note finished, Hasu no Sora performed "Aurora Flower."

Following several minutes of judging, Hasu no Sora Jogakuin School Idol Club was declared the winner.

Izumi apologized for failing to make Ceras a champion.

But Ceras, who had finally fulfilled her dream of standing on the Love Live stage as a Mizukawa school idol, wore a bright and satisfied expression.

With x MEETS appearance in February of the 104th term
On February 6, 2025, Ceras appeared as a surprise guest on With x MEETS.

She was the first person outside the Hasu no Sora school idol club to appear on the program.

She said she wanted to express gratitude to everyone who had helped make her Love Live appearance possible.

She entered with a fake regional accent and joked that Hasu no Sora's victory came from "the power of numbers."

Because the stream ran into the night, viewers questioned how she could possibly have returned home to Nagano in time.

No explanation was given, and before her formal enrollment in the 105th term, this fueled much fan speculation.

Entering Hasu no Sora in the 105th term
In April of the 105th term, Ceras enrolled at Hasu no Sora Jogakuin and joined the school idol club.

She chose the school partly because Kaho attended it, and partly because she was fascinated by the creativity and spirit that had built the Mizuren Festival from nothing.

Izumi, who also transferred in for similar reasons, entered the dormitory with her after arriving by bullet train.

When they learned the club was in the middle of recruitment, Ceras prepared to state her wish to join directly rather than getting swept up in the campaign.

Izumi teased her about being shy and about the difficulty of making friends in an all-dorm school.

Ceras objected, summoned her courage, and knocked on the clubroom door.

The club, at that point facing a frightening possibility of zero new members, welcomed both girls with open arms.

As the club's only first-year student, Ceras was doted on by Kaho and the seniors she had already come to know.

Unit selection and the rebirth of Edel Note
At first, Ceras planned to experience all three traditional Hasu no Sora units before choosing one.

Each unit's new goal after the graduation of the former third-years looked dazzling to her, and she could not decide.

Beside her, Izumi abruptly said she would choose Cerise Bouquet because she could contribute in composition after the unit had lost key creators.

Ceras immediately sensed that this was not Izumi's true desire.

When pressed, Izumi declared that she had no dream of her own.

She said she only felt heat and a sense of being alive while helping other people's dreams come true.

Shaken by this, Ceras nearly chose Dollchestra purely so her own composing could be useful somewhere else.

Kosuzu Kachimachi saw through this and gently encouraged her to act on what she truly wanted.

Ceras then turned to Kaho, now properly calling her "President," and asked permission for a bold act that would break the school's traditional structure.

She went to Izumi and declared, "I will fulfill the dream of the one who fulfilled my dream."

She added that this time Izumi would be the princess.

Her new goal was to make Izumi truly fall in love with being a school idol.

Izumi warned her that this would only be a one-year contract and that she would end up taking Ceras's time in return.

Ceras accepted without hesitation, saying one year would be enough and that she would carve her own name into Izumi's heart forever.

With that, the two revived Edel Note as a limited-time unit.

Once the original purpose of the contract had been fulfilled, the plan was always for the unit to end.

Summer camp in the 105th term
In August, Ceras received an invitation to a joint summer training camp with school idols from other schools.

The connection had grown from the various competitions and interactions she arranged during the previous year while trying to build Mizukawa's path to victory.

For the camp, she proposed a homework assignment: every participant had to finish lyrics for one full song.

Part of her motive was to draw out Izumi's true feelings and see whether Izumi was really beginning to love school idol activity for herself.

She also arranged further trials for Izumi because she wanted to push her to the limit and, admittedly, make her say she had been beaten.

During a relay-style beach marathon against a team of three senior students plus Ceras, they narrowly lost.

The next day, she decided there was no need for a formal contest if they could simply overwhelm Izumi directly.

She even had Ginko try tickling Izumi to force a reaction, only for the plan to backfire.

An attempt by Kaho and Hime Anyoji to overwhelm Izumi with handsome-role acting also failed.

But during a bravery-test event, Izumi froze due to old trauma and finally showed clear weakness.

Ceras did not rejoice at this opening.

Instead, she worried that if fear made Izumi hate school idols, everything would be ruined, so she urged her to withdraw from the event.

Izumi chose to continue on the proper route anyway.

She explained that even if the destination was darkness, standing still was more unbearable to her.

The two finished the event together and even found it fun, despite Ceras causing chaos over cicadas.

By the end of camp, almost everyone had completed their lyric assignment thanks to support from Ceras and the Hasu no Sora members.

Izumi alone remained blank past the deadline.

She was crushed by the realization that even the terror she had felt could not be transformed into a meaningful emotional form.

Encouraged by Ginko, Ceras reached out to her.

She told Izumi that even lyrics born from emptiness and darkness could become school idol songs.

Later, Izumi handed over words she had believed no one would want to hear.

Ceras set them to music, completing the song "Fusion Crust."

September of the 105th term
Ceras helped with Bloom Days, a Kanazawa town revitalization event organized by Ginko.

During the event's stamp rally scenario, "Hasu no Sora Fantasy," she played the role of Princess Ceras from a certain kingdom.

She served as the guide in the introductory segment and again at the climax.

There she narrated the story involving "Vampire Knight Izumi" and the salvation of the world.
