(idol.st)

desc 1:
A member of Hasunosora Girls’ High School’s 102nd Class. While she is a performer often lauded as a genius by those around her, Tsuzuri is a friendly person who takes things at her own pace, has a very peculiar choice of words, and gets lonely easily. Tsuzuri loves school idols, and she is truly proud that she was one of them.

desc 2:
A third-year at Hasunosora Girls’ High. Tsuzuri is a performer often lauded as a genius by those around her. She is a friendly person who takes things at her own pace, has a very peculiar choice of words, and gets lonely easily. While she is not good at expressing herself, she has very strong feelings toward school idols.

desc 3:
Hasunosora 2nd year. She is often absentminded and has a peculiar way of speaking. But on stage, she is an excellent performer. Because of this, people call her a genius and consider her difficult to approach, but in reality, she gets lonely easily and is very friendly.

background:
Before enrolling in Hasunosora, Tsuzuri didn't know much about school idols. She joined the school idol club on a whim, and Kozue Otomune helps her be familiarized with the world of school idols, often watching idol videos together all night. Despite being a total beginner, she would perform brilliantly as a school idol, gaining many fans and even scouted by another school. A panicked Kozue plotted to have Tsuzuri remain in Hasunosora, and as a result, Megumi Fujishima was injured on a live show because their synergy was ruined. Despite proceeding to win the Love Live! regional qualifier and earning the right to perform at the national stage, a rift was created between Tsuzuri and Kozue, causing Hasunosora to drop out from the tournament.

personality:
She is absent-minded, always in a daze, and talks with a peculiar flair, but an excellent performer when on stage. Because of this, people often call her a genius and consider her difficult to approach. Fans even swarm her performance in the hallways when she has them. However, in reality, she is easily lonely and very friendly, resembling a rather clingy child. Because of this demeanor, the rest of the club has become her caretakers, and she loves them endlessly in return. She has a very difficult time explaining herself, and this affects both her teaching and social skills. Her dancing advice is incomprehensible to anyone but Sayaka Murano, and she has immense trouble conveying her inner thoughts and problems.

However, despite her oddities, she is an extremely kind and sensitive person. She excels at listening to others and giving them support, and is able to be a calming presence to those who are troubled. She says that this is fun for her, and that she likes to see people smile after she alleviates their worries.[2]

Regarding Sayaka, she has a great attachment towards her, and the two are often seen together. In WithxMeets on 4/22/23, she outright states that, "My favorite is Saya, and Saya's favorite is me". She has the responsibility of awakening Tsuzuri in the mornings, making her lunch, guiding her around, and other tasks. After she made up with Kozue, she is also quite affectionate towards her, as even though Kozue can be exasperated with her.

She has the habit of shortening people's names to just two characters, calling Sayaka "Saya", Kozue "Kozu", Megumi "Megu", Rurino "Ruri", Kosuzu "Suzu", and Ginko "Gin". Three syllables is too long for her.

A: Tsuzuri, as she admitted herself, is not very good at understanding the feelings of others or expressing them as they are. She has different feeling with many people and often shows socially inappropriate reactions. This is easily overlooked because she is friendly and mild, but it is a feature of Fe inf. On the other hand, there are times when Tsuzuri shows sharp and elaborate thinking, which is closer to the expression of Ti than Si. She is always cared for by Sayaka because she lacks a sense of reality as a whole. Rather than stable data in the past or common sense, Tsuzuri speaks and acts based on her intuition or instantaneous thoughts. This is very far from Si dom. Besides, Te can't be found either.
It is inappropriate to presume that all realists are ISTJs. Even Tsuzuri's realist aspect is not the overall characteristic of the character, but rather an 'unexpected characteristic'!

(anibase.net)

Tsuzuri is a student of the 102nd class at Hasu no Sora Girls' High School and, during her time at school, belonged to the School Idol Club and the unit DOLLCHESTRA.

Her senior in the unit was Sachi Ogami, and her juniors were Sayaka Murano and Kosuzu Kachimachi.

She usually seems spaced out and talks with a highly idiosyncratic sense of phrasing.

Her self-introductions can sound almost nonsensical, which adds to her mysterious image.

On stage, however, she displays exceptional performance ability.

Because of that brilliance and her strange aura, many people see her as an untouchable genius.

In truth, she is lonely at heart and very affectionate.

She is also bad at everyday life, often unable to wake up on time and even forgetting to eat, so Sayaka frequently took care of her daily needs while they were students.

She dreams of supporting others and staying close to them.

After graduation, she goes on to further education with the goal of becoming a teacher.

Tsuzuri speaks in a very flat, subdued tone.

Her delivery is so low-key that even among quiet characters, she stands out.

She gives nicknames to the other members using two consecutive syllables from their names.

This is why Sayaka becomes "Saya" and Kozue Otomune becomes "Kozu," while Kosuzu becomes "Suzu" to avoid confusion.

When she had not yet assigned someone a nickname, she would call them "you."

Even after giving a nickname, she may still use "you" depending on the situation.

Her speech is gender-neutral and often bluntly declarative.

In broadcasts, she often stretches simple words for effect, making her sound soft and oddly playful.

She talks in a childlike, halting way, but because she is also calm, she is hard to read.

Her word choices are unusual, her topics shift constantly, and her actions can be sudden and unpredictable.

She frequently uses strange metaphors, creating what fans think of as her own personal language.

Sayaka initially struggled with it, but after spending time with her every day, she learned to understand even long and abstract statements, while Kosuzu mastered Tsuzuri's fish-themed metaphor logic after about nine months.

When relaxed, her whimsical side becomes especially obvious.

According to an introductory video, if she is handed a disentanglement puzzle, she can stay quiet for half a day without solving it.

She depended heavily on Sayaka for practical life management.

Sayaka woke her up, made her lunch, managed her schedule, and even brought her to practice.

Her practical working ability is also weak.

At part-time jobs assigned as part of club training, she would play with merchandise, suddenly drift into philosophy, or get caught up in talking to customers, so she mostly ended up being used as a customer magnet thanks to her idol popularity.

Yet whenever the task involves artistry, her ability becomes striking.

She can make shaved ice, plated food, and painted crafts with such originality and quality that others describe them as expressing the very spirit of Kanazawa.

She often draws a penguin-like mascot as a stand-in for herself.

She is especially fond of a chicken doll in the clubroom called Peking Duck, and she also likes to draw penguins with rooster combs.

According to a stream, she originally brought that doll in from the school courtyard.

She even remembered that it originally had a ribbon instead of a crest-like decoration.

She later developed an interest in fishing after trying it in the freezing Sea of Japan region and enjoying it much more than expected.

She also showed strong performance in a competitive puzzle game taught to her by Hime Anyoji.

She excels at mathematics because she likes that answers are fixed and certain.

She also turns out to be good at information processing and once calculated data transmission amounts instantly by hand and calculator.

By contrast, language-based subjects are her weak point.

Modern literature, classical literature, and history are all difficult for her, and one application form she filled out simply gave the reason for use as "practice."

Even so, she always finishes her summer homework.

She is not unsociable, though, and talks readily even with strangers, which made her popular at Omicho Market and around school.

As a first-year student, she could not even say thank you.

After meeting Sayaka, she gradually learned to do so naturally and began giving small gifts such as shiny acorns or cup noodles as tokens of gratitude or celebration.

Unexpectedly, she is also good at acting.

Because she memorizes well, she can deliver long lines with emotional force once she has learned them properly.

In truly important moments, she can communicate in a clear and ordinary way.

She can approach unfamiliar people without fear, and when she feels something is unjust, she is capable of questioning it very logically.

Her emergency response skills are less impressive than her artistic ones.

When Kosuzu fell into Lake Hasu, Tsuzuri could only follow after Sayaka into the water, and when Sayaka collapsed with a fever, Tsuzuri froze for a few seconds before asking Kaho Hinoshita for instructions and preparing clean clothes.

Her friendliness shows clearly in how she treats other members.

She quickly played with the new first-years, eagerly copied Megumi Fujishima's cheerful greetings, and often amused herself in broadcasts by acting out what others were talking about in the background.

She has no problem doing cute poses or lines.

Sometimes she even pushes Sayaka into embarrassing situations just to enjoy watching her react.

The dining hall's chocolate cake became a regular item because Tsuzuri requested it so often.

From the beginning of her first year, she kept asking whether there was chocolate cake that day, and the staff learned her face and name immediately.

She likes more than the foods listed in her official profile.

Her favorite side dishes include Kanazawa-style oden and fried chicken, her favorite fish is grilled salmon, and she also enjoys vegetables, small snack foods, ice cream, and sentimental boxed sweets tied to personal memories.

Despite eating sweets every day, she somehow stays slim.

She and Hime are also snack companions who often meet at the school shop.

She does not seem to know much about monster-collecting game franchises.

On the school trip to Tokyo, she wanted to buy a big pink creature plush at a themed store but had to settle for a smaller one due to budget.
