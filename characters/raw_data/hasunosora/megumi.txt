(idol.st)

desc 1:
A member of Hasunosora Girls’ High School’s 102nd Class. Megumi worked in show business from a young age, so she is aware of her cuteness. She spares no effort in working toward her goal of captivating the world. On the other hand, her academic ability is dreadful because she doesn’t do anything she doesn’t care about. After graduating high school, Megumi took off to see the world in order to fulfill her dream.

desc 2:
A third-year at Hasunosora Girls’ High. As a former child actor, Megumi is very aware of her own cuteness. She spares no effort to look cute every day in order to make the world go crazy for “Mira-Cra Park!”. On the other hand, because she only does things she wants to, her academics are terrible.

desc 3:
A Hasunosora second-year. She has been working in the entertainment industry from a young age, so she is aware of her cuteness and is perfectly willing to work hard to be seen as such. However, since she never does things she is not interested in, she was forced to enroll in the school because of her poor academic performance. Due to certain circumstances, she is taking a break from school idol activities.

background:
Rurino and her are childhood friends, and have known each other from a young age. As the older one, she tried to be the cooler, more experienced senpai, taking on big challenges such as becoming a child actress just to impress Rurino. She did everything she could to portray herself as perfect and dazzling, hiding the many auditions and setbacks she had actually faced to get there.

Because of her horrible school performance, she was forced to take a break from the entertainment industry and enroll at Hasunosora, where she joined the school idol club as a first year. She performed in Mira-Cra Park! with Sachi before the Love Live! regional qualifier, where she fell off the stage and was forced to quit the club. She spent a long time in rehab, and although her injury is healed, she is still psychologically scarred from the incident and can't bring herself to dance on stage. She has spent the remainder of her first year and half of her second year doing livestreams from her room instead.

Before attending Hasunosora, her mother made her promise to become "a fine and sensible person" before graduating. Her mother, while usually kind, can be very scary when angered, and is known to lecture for hours on end.

personality:
She is self-conscious of her own cuteness, so she spares no effort in order to be seen as cute. This can be seen in her creation of "ハロめぐ/Hello Megu", the unique greeting she's made for herself, along with it's variants (such as "バイめぐ/Bye Megu"). During this greeting, she sticks her right arm out and winks her right eye, making it her signature pose. Her academic performance is poor since she absolutely won't do things she's not interested in, and she doesn't even know that the word "proofs" relates to math. She spends almost every day in supplementary classes due to failing almost every test. In regards to her favorite subject, she simply says "It's okay to not study because it won't help you in the future".

She and Rurino Osawa are friends since their middle school. Being a year older, she strives to be ahead of Rurino, wanting to appear as the cool, older girl. However, despite her gravitas, she holds fear and insecurity deep in her heart. She can become quite upset when losing, with Rurino needing to calm her down lest she spiral into depression or further anger.

(anibase.net)

In front of fans, Megumi usually switches into an adorable idol mode.

She is bright, talkative, and naturally good at pulling people in with cute gestures and clever remarks.

Her talking skills are one of her strengths.

At one point, she even kept things running through conversation alone when a live show started late.

In private, however, she is much more blunt and cocky.


She speaks casually, often in a rougher tone, and tends to tease the people around her.

Outside of school idol work, fan service, beauty care, and having fun, her daily habits are not exactly diligent.

She dislikes improving in areas she finds boring and often jokes around instead.

She especially enjoys provoking Kozue.

Even her juniors sometimes lose patience with her unserious side, but she is also very caring and dependable when others truly need help.

Megumi takes self-care seriously and never neglects beauty work.

She scolds Rurino for not doing makeup properly and often gives beauty advice to the other members, with cosmetics becoming one of her signature motifs.

She loves shopping, claims to be a regular at Laforet Harajuku, and even visited accessory shops during school trips.

She is also highly skilled with devices and editing, using those talents for both school idol activities and her personal channel.

She taught editing and related technical skills to Rurino and Hime.

In Kosuzu Kachimachi's self-produced film during the 104th term, Megumi handled filming and directing.

She enjoys games and is very comfortable with Minecraft, including in livestreams.

On the other hand, she was a complete beginner at first-person shooters until she met Hime.

She is also a talented illustrator.

She can draw polished anime-style portraits of club members even casually on a whiteboard during live broadcasts.

Her symbolic image is an angel, matching her member color.

When drawing animal icons for herself, she often uses a shepherd dog, reflecting her fondness for German Shepherds.

She loves sweets, especially Japanese confections.

Another favorite not listed in her profile is matcha doughnuts, and she often takes other members to cafes such as Starbucks.

She also likes meat and fish, especially negitoro and salmon.

She can decorate store-bought sponge cake, but she rarely cooks for herself, which leaves her dorm refrigerator nearly empty.

Megumi is bad with money.

Rurino has so little faith in her financial judgment that she flatly told Megumi never to try stock trading.

She also dislikes insects.

In one side work, she is shown avoiding cicadas and ants.

Underneath her playful exterior, she has a fierce temper when faced with unfairness or sadness.


When she snaps, her voice drops and she can start shouting, though Kozue says this is partly just how Megumi vents stress before getting back on her feet.

She also has a strong sense of justice and cannot ignore it when friends get hurt.

Her weakness is that she cannot resist someone pleading with her using a puppy-like expression, which she believes may be connected to her affection for dogs.

Her ideal school idol is someone cute and cool who keeps running forward at full speed.

She believes she should be the kind of ultimate school idol others admire, and she proudly calls Miracle Park! the strongest unit with Rurino and Hime.

Although Megumi says being club president would not suit her because she is not the hardworking type, Kozue has said Megumi might actually have become a good leader.

That impression reportedly collapsed after a first-year midterm exam revealed just how bad Megumi was academically.

Rurino says Megumi is naturally smart.

Even so, her actual grades are terrible.

Her mother made her attend Hasu no Sora partly to force some basic education into her.

The results are mixed at best.

She misuses idioms, struggles with kanji reading, gave up helping with paperwork, makes plans full of holes, and once failed to finish summer homework until September.

According to Rurino, Megumi keeps focusing on practice even right before make-up exams.

She reacts badly to words like study and test.

Still, she is willing to learn if the topic interests her, such as digital music production.

Among the Great Triangle, she is the lyric writer.

She wrote lyrics for songs such as Embracing Petals, and songwriting seems to sharpen her vocabulary in ways ordinary studying never could.

Her reputation for bad academics became such a running joke that an April Fool's prank about her claimed she had been held back a year.

There is even an in-school rumor about a legendary student called the Queen of the Remedial Room, and Megumi laughed at it without realizing it was essentially about her.

Megumi joined the School Idol Club from her first year.

However, before Love Live!, she was injured during a school festival stage performance and fell from the stage.

Her leg eventually healed, but the incident left her traumatized.

Although she could dance during practice, she would freeze on stage and become unable to perform properly.

She felt crushed by the idea that others had to cover for her.

Eventually, she told Kozue and Tsuzuri she should stop trying to return.

As a result, Kozue and Tsuzuri entered Love Live! without her, though later circumstances led the three of them to discuss things and withdraw from the national competition.

The second-year trio then drifted apart and agreed to keep their distance for a while.

During this period, from the autumn of her first year to August of her second year, Megumi was effectively on hiatus from the club.

She still appeared as an individual streamer and ran her own visible online activities.


Everything changed when Rurino came back from California and transferred into Hasu no Sora.

Megumi had hidden the truth about her injury and trauma from her childhood friend, even as Rurino had become interested in school idols because of her.

Rurino came to the school largely for Megumi's sake.

Megumi tried to push her away, supposedly for Rurino's future, but Rurino's determination eventually broke through her defenses.

A possible path as a vocals-only school idol was discussed.

Megumi rejected it because giving up dance meant giving up something deeply tied to her bond with Rurino.

At an August live event, Rurino performed what was framed as a live shared by the two of them, even though she stood there alone.

Watching from the audience, Megumi could not stay away when she realized Rurino was waiting for her.

She ran onto the stage, danced with Rurino, and succeeded.

That moment allowed her to overcome her trauma and officially return to school idol activities.

The two then announced the rebirth of the traditional unit Miracle Park!.

This return is one of the biggest turning points in her story.

Megumi's dream is to make the whole world fall under her spell.

As a child, she imagined that dream while playing with Rurino.

After becoming a school idol, that dream shifted into captivating the whole world from Hasu no Sora.

She carried that ambition into Love Live! competition.

After a defeat on the final stage, she was furious and frustrated.

But instead of breaking, she doubled down and swore Miracle Park! would rise again.

At first, she planned to return to talent work after graduation.

In the end, however, she refused to live on a path others had laid out for her.

She chose instead to train overseas as a performer.

After graduation, she left alone for California to pursue the dream of moving audiences around the world.

Although others worried about her language skills and daily life abroad, later story material shows that she is managing.


She reappears from California while looking ahead to an even wider future for school idols.

In her first year, Megumi was rougher, sharper, and more openly rebellious than she is later.

She had been forced into Hasu no Sora and looked down on the school at first.

She joined the School Idol Club partly out of boredom and partly to resist the path others expected from her.

She bragged about her talent career, acted more selfishly, and pushed for songs that would make her stand out.

Her early streaming persona was also very different.

In fact, her first-year broadcast archives were hidden after her injury, partly because of the accident and partly because she considered them embarrassing black history.

Back then, she spoke more slowly, laughed awkwardly, pretended not to be good with tech, and generally approached the streams in a shallow way.

Watching that old version of herself later was physically painful for her.

Still, her time in Miracle Park! with Sachi taught her something crucial.

She realized that performing was not just about being cute, but about hearing people's joy up close and turning that into a dream.

Even in that early period, she had talent.

She once won a monthly MVP distinction on School Idol Connect.

103rd Term

For much of the early 103rd-term story, Megumi appears only occasionally.

She serves more as a streamer and observer than an active club member.

Her past is gradually explained by Kozue, Tsuzuri, and Rurino.

The full picture reveals the injury, trauma, and emotional fallout that caused her hiatus.

Once she returns, she quickly throws herself back into things.

She tries to tighten the relaxed atmosphere in the club and takes charge of a major hot spring inn promotional event.

That event becomes messy after a promotional video she edited succeeds too well and attracts far more visitors than expected.

Megumi feels responsible and tries to shoulder everything alone until the others help resolve the crisis.

Later, she clashes badly with Rurino over the kind of activity Miracle Park! should pursue.

Their conflict eventually feeds into shuffle unit projects and pushes both of them to confront their own weaknesses.

Megumi forms Kaho-Megu Gelato with Kaho Hinoshita.

While she has fun making a bright, cute-centered performance, she is shaken by the quality of the others' work and nearly runs away emotionally before reconciling with Rurino.

She also becomes the unit's lyric-focused member and helps write new material for later performances.

This side of her creative talent gains more importance over time.

104th Term

In the 104th term, Megumi wants Miracle Park! to gain another member.

Using lessons learned from her previous conflict with Rurino, she pushes for growth rather than clinging too tightly.

That leads to Hime Anyoji joining the unit.

Megumi and Rurino recruit her after discovering she is both a strong admirer of Miracle Park! and a famous streamer.

During the Nadeshiko Festival, Megumi aims for Miracle Park! to win the top prize.

Unfortunately, she spends most of the preparation period stuck in remedial study sessions.

This leaves Rurino and Hime handling much of the planning.

When hidden feelings and misunderstandings surface, Megumi helps both of them confront what they really want and encourages more honest communication within the trio.

Later, when Hime falls into a slump over balancing games and school idol life, Megumi offers a very Megumi-like answer.

She suggests that if Hime loves many things, then she should simply add even more things she loves.

Megumi introduces Hime to guitar and nudges her toward exploring wider interests.

In the end, Hime realizes what she truly loves is doing things together with other people, which helps her recover.

Megumi still strongly resents having been forced into a school life focused on studying.

Even so, she becomes serious about graduating.

Her final major conflict comes when her mother reminds her of an old promise: by the time she graduates, she must become a respectable, well-rounded person.

Because Megumi has repeatedly hidden the truth about her lack of progress, she fears being locked down at home after graduation.

The club members rally around her and hold intense cram sessions in general knowledge, manners, and home economics.

Thanks to that support, Megumi gathers the strength to face her mother honestly.

A painful revelation involving her mother's own dreams nearly breaks her.

But with support, especially a sharp push from Kozue, Megumi decides to carry all the pain and sadness and still deliver joy.

She then focuses on creating the song Key of Like! for the regional competition.

She sends her mother a video invitation to watch the performance, and the group successfully advances.

In another later episode, she works with Hime and Izumi Katsuragi to produce a public-relations video for another school.

This highlights her technical ability and collaborative spirit.

Megumi graduates from Hasu no Sora alongside Kozue and Tsuzuri at the end of the 104th term.

Shortly before graduation, she performs her final solo stream.

105th Term and Beyond

After graduating, Megumi goes to California alone.

She does this to continue training as a performer and chase her dream on a global scale.

She later reappears in story material from abroad, shown thinking about her old friends and the future awaiting school idols.

On December 4, she also returns to Hallo-Megu Channel for a long-awaited stream, showing fans her face again.
