background:
Returning from studying abroad in California, she is enrolling at Hasunosora as a first-year student. She and Megumi Fujishima have been childhood friends since elementary school, with Rurino looking up to her as the cool, older kid capable of doing anything.

As a child, she spent all of her energy making sure that her friends didn't fight. Originally, this was just when they were arguing, but eventually she started doing it all the time. It started to weigh on her, as she spent all her time wondering if people were getting bored or tired and treating it as her responsibility to make them happy again. Because of this pressure, she started to spend more and more time alone, eventually realizing just how fun solitude could be.

personality:
She is a cheerful and energetic girl, able to find enjoyment in anything and anyone. However, she must always consider her "battery", a sort of social battery that depletes quickly. If this gets too low, then she loses all her energy and ability to think, being unable to communicate properly and appearing extremely lethargic and sluggish. In order to preserve this energy, she has to quickly find time to herself, treating it as a recharging period. Because of her energy's short limit, she enjoys solitary activities and places in nature.

Because of her time in California, she claims that she can speak English. However, in fact, her English is not very good, and is something that only Kaho is impressed by.

Her favorite phrase is "The heart that does not want to enter that path is your own personal master". This saying is included in a traditional tea ceremony guide, and it means that it's difficult to continue something unenjoyable just because others tell you to. In order to venture upon a path, you must want to do it and take an initiative in it, and this mindset becomes one's master in this analogy.

Since she spends much of her energy trying to make others happy, she pays the most attention to how others are feeling. She's very perceptive about this and will notice right away if someone is feeling down.

(anibase.net)

On the surface, Rurino looks like a flashy, party-loving extrovert who loves fun above all else.

She is bright, energetic, and good at becoming friends with almost anyone.

In reality, she may be the kindest and most considerate member of the cast.

She constantly watches how other people are feeling and instinctively tries to keep everyone around her comfortable and included.

That habit is so strong that it drains her mentally.

When she overuses that energy, she enters the state she calls battery depletion.

She is not especially academic, but she is still above the level of the club’s weaker students.

She does not pile up summer homework and never ends up in remedial classes.

She worries about Megumi’s habit of avoiding study.

Even when she usually sides with Megumi’s dramatic ideas, she quickly pushes back if those ideas sound reckless for the future.

Rurino’s speech is casual, playful, and packed with slang.

Her tone shifts constantly, and she often sounds performative in a funny way.

She calls Megumi “Megu-chan,” and Megumi calls her “Ruri-chan.”

She first gives Hime a nickname to get closer to her, then later drops the nickname and begins calling her simply Hime as a sign that they have become true partners.

Her naming style for seniors is unusually freeform.

She gives Kozue Otomune and Tsuzuri Yugiri a variety of playful nicknames rather than using only standard respectful titles.

Her voice became especially famous among fans for its rough, emphatic delivery.

People often joke that even her spoken lines sound like they come with extra voiced marks attached.

She loves outdoor activities and enjoys stretching out physically in open spaces.

Because of her fishing hobby, she loves the sea even in the cold winter waters of the Sea of Japan.

From the start of her school idol life, she already has strong stamina, balance, and athletic ability.

She has dance experience from lessons and self-training, and she is also good at basketball from practicing by herself overseas.

She draws well.

She is also a gamer, especially fond of first-person shooters, which she once referred to with a playful alternate name.

She enjoys ghost stories and even hosts spooky themed streams.

Her favorite extra foods include sweet vegetables like pumpkin, burgers, and potato chips.

She seems to dislike green peppers.

That detail is casually revealed by Megumi.

She owns plenty of Chow Chow goods.

Her name is derived from lapis lazuli, which ties neatly into her member color and overall image.

Although her English conversation is functional enough to get by, her pronunciation is extremely accented and her vocabulary is simple.

Megumi openly teases her about whether her English ever really worked.

Rurino’s own theory of language exchange is pure passion.

She once suggests that coming at people with enough energy matters more than trying to sound perfect.

She likes difficult Japanese-style sayings and old expressions far more than her English skill would suggest.

That contrast is one of her funniest traits.

Rurino’s most famous character trait is her tendency to run out of emotional charge.

After spending too much effort caring for others, she suddenly drops from upbeat mode into a gloomy, exhausted state.

When depleted, she becomes passive, downcast, and likely to flee from people.

She even mutters things like, “Ruri is a plankton.”

In early promotional videos and commercials, she is shown getting breathless after her own high-energy self-introduction.

She describes the process as losing her internal “Ruri points.”

Megumi explains that once Rurino hits that limit, she basically cannot move until she gets time alone.

Being by herself is how she recharges.

Interestingly, her battery does not drain when she is with Megumi.

Large events like live performances are still tiring, but ordinary time together feels naturally safe to her.

Later, after receiving a large cardboard box from her friends, she discovers that hiding inside it helps her recharge even when other people are nearby.

This leads to the absurd but memorable image of the others trying to hold conversations with a talking cardboard box.

During one livestream, she experiences battery depletion on camera and keeps participating while boxed up.

That moment becomes important because she openly shares her insecurity with the audience.

This trait began in elementary school.

Rurino loved lively, happy spaces so much that she made it her personal mission to protect them.

If someone looked bored, upset, or irritated, she would work hard to cheer them up.

Over time, constantly managing the mood of a group became second nature.

She also hated the idea of people learning about her weakness and then worrying about her.

So she taught herself to disappear before fully breaking down.

Eventually she discovered the fun of being alone.

That turned into a love of solo activities such as going to batting cages alone, fishing alone, and even eating barbecue alone.

Because she dislikes hurting or excluding people, she has trouble with direct competition.

At first she avoids serious game sessions with Hime because Hime is far too intense.

Over time, especially after the first-year Love Live! challenge and the second-year Nadeshiko Festival, she learns to enjoy both victory and earnest competition.

Her personal battery capacity also grows stronger through repeated emotional training.

This growth is especially noticeable at major turning points: her California study period, the start of full second-year activity, the end of her second year, and her key 105th-term development.

Fans often cite her as one of the cast members with the biggest gap between first impression and true personality.

Ironically, although many viewers initially misread her as a teasing brat type, the opposite is mostly true.

She only leans into that style as a joke, and doing so burns through her energy at top speed.

Hime eventually gives her the grandiose title “the saint of universal love born in the modern age.”

The rest of the cast finds this so funny that the phrase spreads.

Rurino is highly versatile and curious.

She throws herself into hobbies with enthusiasm and tends to find joy in almost everything.

She is strong enough physically to keep up with demanding performances.

Her stamina, coordination, and body control are repeatedly highlighted.

She is musically creative as well.

After forming a shuffle unit with Tsuzuri, she begins composing with an acoustic guitar because she wants to express herself through her own sound.

She names that guitar Sazanami-chan, or “Little Ripple.”

The name is chosen through listener suggestions on a livestream, mirroring how Megumi once named her own cherished item.

At the crossover event Dimension Festival: I Love Song Contest, she receives the new nickname “Ruripi” from Ai Miyashita of another series entry during the intermission talk segment.

For Rurino, who is the junior there, it becomes a fun special moment.

After graduation, Rurino decides to aim for a career as a solo artist.

This becomes the clearest statement of her independent dream.

It is a fitting destination for someone who began by following another person’s light.

By the end of her arc, she is ready to shine on her own.


103rd Term

At the start of the main story, Rurino is still living in California and has no direct involvement with Hasu no Sora.

During the first stretch of the narrative, the story instead follows Kaho Hinoshita, Sayaka Murano, and the members of the other units.

Megumi appears around that time as a side character doing talk-focused solo activity through her streaming channel.

Rurino, however, has no comparable early spotlight.

She first appears in an interlude after episode 6.

There, her California life and her decision to investigate Hasu no Sora and Megumi’s school idol club are shown.

She properly enters the story in episode 9.

After returning to Japan for the first time in about a year and a half, she transfers into Hasu no Sora as a first-year student just before summer vacation.

She immediately visits the school idol club and starts with a trial membership.

Because she has only just returned from overseas, the club president recommends easing her in gradually.

During practice, her battery depletion causes a breakdown and she runs away, which exposes her hidden side to Kaho and Sayaka.

Feeling that her nature may not fit the club, she tries to quit.

Kaho and Sayaka accept her exactly as she is.

That acceptance convinces her to stay, and from then on she becomes very close with both of them.

The reason she came all the way back from abroad is Megumi.

Megumi is the person she admires most, and watching the club’s streams because of Megumi is what made her dream of becoming a school idol too.

At that time, Megumi has stopped performing and withdrawn from the club.

When the older students reveal why, Rurino decides she will do whatever she can to bring Megumi back.

Rurino and Megumi have been close since early childhood.

They know each other’s embarrassing stories and old dreams.

As children, Megumi was cool, talented, and good at everything, and Rurino wanted to chase after her.

Their shared dream was to make the whole world obsessed with them, especially through dance.

Then Rurino moved away.

Back then she was bad at making friends.

To keep cheering her up even from afar, Megumi became a child performer so that Rurino could always see her on television.

In return, when Rurino gradually found her footing, she gave Megumi a pair of shoes.

Rurino later went to California as a kind of self-imposed training journey.

She wanted to become someone who could stand on her own even without Megumi nearby, and the experience increased her emotional stamina.

Megumi’s reason for becoming a school idol was tied to how much fun the two of them had together and how that joy spread to others.

It was Megumi who first invited Rurino into the world of Hasu no Sora school idols.

Megumi eventually lashes out and cruelly rejects Rurino’s efforts because trauma from a leg injury has left her unable to return to the stage.

Rurino refuses to back down.

After learning that Megumi still keeps the old shoes, Rurino declares that this time she will be the one pulling Megumi forward.

She even announces a live with Megumi as part of the lineup and stands on stage alone first.

In the end, Megumi overcomes her trauma and returns to the club.

Together they form Mira-Cra Park!, aiming to make the whole world obsessed with them.

At first, Rurino’s main reason for school idol activity is simple: fun, and the fact that Megumi makes it look fun.

Even when asked why she wants to compete in Love Live!, she initially frames it as something enjoyable because it means being active with Megumi.

Then Hasu no Sora loses in the Love Live! final.

Rurino is shaken, not just because of defeat, but because she realizes she had still been watching the competition from a distance instead of fully investing herself as a true school idol.

Kozue Otomune helps her recognize that she did want victory, and that her effort had been real all along.

From there, she starts thinking more seriously about the people who support them.

Episode 17 becomes a major turning point.

Rurino streams while fully in depleted mode and discovers that some fans genuinely like the calm, quiet atmosphere she creates in that state.

Later, when Mira-Cra Park! discusses its future, Megumi proposes loud, energetic crowd-pleasing events.

Rurino proposes gentler ideas like napping under the blue sky or a fishing-pond activity, arguing that they should also reach people who do not have the energy to get excited.

Megumi rejects this and even claims that Rurino is only humoring school idol work instead of truly doing it for herself.

The argument explodes into a huge fight.

To prove her own vision, Rurino suggests a shuffle-unit live and ends up paired with Tsuzuri Yugiri in a group with a comedic long-form name.

Because Tsuzuri is exactly the kind of person Rurino instinctively tiptoes around, working together is difficult but revealing.

Rurino develops a one-person-at-a-time approach.

She introduces fishing because it suits Tsuzuri and begins shaping a style centered on meeting each person where they are.

At the shuffle stage, she slows the performance down, waits for the audience’s penlight colors to change, talks directly with the crowd, sits with them, and promises that anyone who feels low can be brought to them and will be given a happy memory.

That stage proves her growth and the value of her philosophy.

Megumi, who had assumed Rurino would fail, instead becomes consumed by inferiority and panic.

She runs away from the dorm and talks about disbanding Mira-Cra Park! and going back overseas.

Rurino catches up to her at a bus stop and finally scolds her.

She tells Megumi that everything she has done was possible because of Megumi, and that only the two of them together can truly create the place they dream about.

They reconcile.

Soon afterward, the two land in trouble with the student council after carrying out an overly chaotic Valentine chocolate stunt.

104th Term

By the start of the 104th term, Rurino has clearly found her own school idol dream.

When Megumi suggests adding a new member to Mira-Cra Park!, Rurino initially resists because she wants the unit to remain just the two of them.

Megumi explains what she learned from the shuffle-unit experience, and Rurino agrees.

The new recruit turns out to be Hime Anyoji, a game streamer Rurino already knows and a devoted fan who comments on their streams.

Rurino and Hime share an interest in first-person shooters, but Hime is far more competitive.

Because Rurino prefers to enjoy games casually, she initially avoids playing seriously with her.

Episode 3 of this arc focuses on Rurino and Hime during the Nadeshiko Festival.

The unit wants the top prize for their event, but Megumi spends most of the preparation period stuck in remedial study, leaving Rurino and Hime to carry the planning and setup.

Rurino has long wanted to do a radio-style attraction for the festival.

However, she silently gives up on that wish because she thinks trying to combine it with Hime’s proposed mini-live and game tournament would be too much.

Hime later learns this and feels awful for unknowingly crushing Rurino’s dream.

Megumi points out that although Rurino can be honest with Megumi, Kaho, and Sayaka, she still has not yet fully let Hime into that inner circle.

Rurino then admits something important.

Even after agreeing in principle to add a new member, part of her feared that if Mira-Cra Park! stopped being just “Megu and Ruri,” it would stop being the strongest version of itself.

But by this point, Hime has already changed her mind through sheer effort and sincerity.

Rurino realizes that the three of them can become the strongest together if they stop holding back.

To repair things, Rurino challenges Hime in Hime’s own favorite shooter.

She trains hard despite not enjoying the one-sided losses.

In the middle of their matches, Rurino tells Hime that Hime’s seriousness about games is the same kind of seriousness she and Megumi bring to Mira-Cra Park!

She wants Hime beside them for both the hardship and the joy of doing everything they really want to do.

By a lucky break, Rurino manages to win a game.

That opens the door for Hime to confess her own worries, and they reconcile.

The festival ends up including the radio idea after all.

From then on, Rurino and Hime begin gaming together much more naturally.

When Hime later falls into a slump, Rurino notices even her dropping game performance through playing with her.

Megumi proposes that Hime should expand the number of things she loves rather than narrowing them.

Rurino takes Hime fishing and talks with her there.

That shared experience becomes one of the keys to helping Hime recover.

A later flashback shows the very beginning of Rurino and Megumi’s friendship in more detail.

Their mothers’ busy careers, their loneliness, and the lapis lazuli necklace all deepen the emotional context of their bond.

As Megumi approaches graduation, Rurino publicly shouts about how sad she is that Megumi will be leaving.

After that outburst, however, she acts more composed than many of the others while the seniors’ graduation gets closer.

On Megumi’s last night at the academy, after the three Mira-Cra Park! members stay up late together, Megumi gives Rurino a present.

It is a platinum ring with lapis lazuli, sized for the ring finger of her right hand.

After the graduation ceremony, Rurino only wears this ring for especially important moments.

At ordinary times, she keeps it carefully stored among her accessories in the dorm.

The ring later becomes a real-world accessory associated with the character and cast.

That detail shows how iconic the gift becomes.

105th Term

Rurino advances to her third year and becomes one of the senior supports of the club.

The new club leadership is a dual-president system headed by Kaho and Sayaka, and Rurino settles into a role where she backs both of them up.

With Megumi gone, Hime becomes her new partner.

For the 105th-term version of Mira-Cra Park!, the unit decides to focus strongly on broadcasting and online delivery so they can offer even more fun than before.

By this point, Rurino’s greeting in streams has evolved into a new personal catchphrase.

Hime, meanwhile, spends the early part of the term still emotionally wrecked by Megumi’s graduation and the end of the old Megumi-Rurino duo.

In episode 2, Hime finally starts stabilizing and throws herself into her own dream.

That dream is tied to promoting her favorite first-person shooter game.

When the staff of a new game announcement event ask Hime for help, she requests support from the school idol club.

She appoints Rurino as the player for the exhibition match, a major featured part of the event.

Rurino accepts because she has spent nearly a year being trained through play by Hime.

Hime studies replay footage and character abilities while coaching Rurino intensely.

During this preparation, Hime quietly asks whether Rurino merely rode along on Megumi’s dream of making the world obsessed with them.

She asks this because she sees parallels with her own insecurity toward Kaho as a leader.

Rurino answers honestly.

At the time, she says, she had no dream of her own, so Megumi’s dream naturally became their dream together.

She adds that once her own direction started forming, she and Megumi clashed many times before reaching mutual understanding.

This answer matters deeply for Hime’s own growth.

Thanks to all the training, Rurino performs well in the serious competition setting of the event itself.

The event succeeds, and Hime moves on to seek a new dream within the school idol club by setting Kaho up as her rival.

That success, however, also leaves a shadow over Rurino’s face.

Her own future path is beginning to come into focus.

Episode 3 is one of Rurino’s major spotlight stories in the 105th term.

The club is searching for a venue that school idols can freely use as a live space.

Kaho enters a rock festival because the prize is a stage they can use for a set period.

But as the group starts composing in an unfamiliar rock style, Kaho catches a cold and goes down.

With one president missing and the usual mood-maker absent, the club begins to sag.

Sayaka then tries to hold everyone together by doing a bizarre imitation of Kaho, which unexpectedly becomes a bizarre but effective emotional support system for the whole group.

Kaho recovers, the club unites around that absurd shared momentum, and they compete in the rock festival.

They still fail to win.

Sayaka blames herself.

Kaho tries to act cheerful, and the strain eventually boils over into a fight between the two presidents.

Rurino tracks them down, but neither will tell her the full truth.

Both basically insist that they do not want to burden her, precisely because she is so kind and because they know about her battery depletion.

This crushes her.

She wants to support them too, and she hates being treated as someone too fragile to rely on.

After the conflict is resolved through help from the younger members, Rurino takes action on her own.

During the Nadeshiko Festival preparation period, she starts a one-woman service project called Handywoman Ruri, taking anonymous requests from a suggestion box and helping with anything.

The requests flood in.

Rurino ends up racing all over campus, handling around a hundred jobs.

She starts this project because the fight between Kaho and Sayaka made her realize that waiting quietly is not enough if she truly wants to help people.

She wants to become stronger, even if that means becoming intrusive.

She helps with meetings, personal practice support for club members, and other difficult tasks.

Eventually she collapses from exhaustion and has to be carried away in cardboard mode.

Right after that, a major scheduling mess breaks out on the student council side.

Rurino drags her depleted body back into action, calls Hime for backup, and helps sort things out by speaking directly and personally with each of the students involved.

At the end of this ordeal, she gathers Kaho and Sayaka on the rooftop.

There she confirms what she already suspects: they avoided depending on her not because she was kind in some abstract way, but because they feared pushing her into battery depletion.

They apologize for how they treated her during their fight.

Rurino responds that she wants to become someone they trust even in their worst moments.

Even if she becomes overbearing, even if she hits battery depletion, she still wants to be their hero.

That declaration becomes one of her defining third-year statements.

Sayaka hesitates but finally agrees to stop hovering over Rurino and to let her take responsibility for herself.

Kaho has a harder time accepting this, so Rurino compares their relationship to Kaho’s own bond with Kozue.

As the 103rd-class trio rebuilds their relationship, Kaho says they need a team name.

Rurino instantly comes up with Hasu no Sanrenga, roughly meaning a three-part lotus bloom.

After this new trio begins, Rurino makes an important private promise with Hime in her room.

Because she expects the new path to demand even more confrontation and emotional strain, she tells Hime that if she ever reaches her limit, she wants Hime to be the one who fully supports her.

Hime accepts, insisting they are equals.

As a sign of that bond, Rurino stops using Hime’s nickname and begins calling her simply Hime.

Hime becomes the only person Rurino addresses that way.

The shift clearly marks her as Rurino’s new equal partner.

The next day, Rurino unusually pushes for a new song to symbolize their restart at the Nadeshiko Festival.

The idea is accepted.

Later, she quickly proves herself as a hero figure by helping restore confidence to a troubled student.

According to Ceras, her popularity among first-year students jumps sharply that summer.

Episode 10 gives her final major spotlight of the term.

When Megumi returns to Japan after a song of hers goes massively viral, Rurino notices that Megumi is struggling with what to make next and offers to compose a song for her.

She throws herself into chasing the idea of virality, involves the whole club, watches the Love Live! final in Tokyo to stir up inspiration, and completes a song after street performance experiments.

Megumi sings it, and it does indeed go viral.

But the people who loved the older, gentler Rurino do not respond well.

The song reveals that in growing stronger, she has drifted away from the heart that once reached the weak and tired.

She collapses emotionally back into her cardboard refuge.

Hime snaps her out of it by confronting her directly.

Rurino retraces her own history and comes to understand that both her strong self and her weak self are irreplaceable.

From that realization she completes Echoes Beyond.

When Megumi leaves for America again, the two confirm that they will not pursue the exact same path after graduation.

Instead, each will aim on her own to make the world obsessed with her art, trusting that their roads will cross again someday.
